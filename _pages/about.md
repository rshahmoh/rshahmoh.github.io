My name is Dr. René Shahmohamadloo and I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Washington State University working for [Dr. Seth Rudman](https://scholar.google.ca/citations?user=tkLbIiAAAAAJ&hl=en). I held a [Liber Ero Postdoctoral Fellow](https://liberero.ca) and [NSERC Postdoctoral Fellow](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/students-etudiants/pd-np/pdf-bp_eng.asp) to work with [Dr. Seth Rudman](https://scholar.google.ca/citations?user=tkLbIiAAAAAJ&hl=en) and [Dr. John Fryxell](https://scholar.google.ca/citations?user=mq20LGsAAAAJ&hl=en). I completed my PhD under [Dr. Paul Sibley](https://scholar.google.ca/citations?user=n4SioRQAAAAJ&hl=en).

Earth’s soils and waters are now pervasively influenced by anthropogenic contaminants and pose ongoing challenges for biodiversity and environmental sustainability. **My research addresses a central question: when do anthropogenic stressors lead to predictable biological responses that can be mitigated, and when do they push populations toward resistance evolution, demographic collapse, or ecological disruption?**

**My research program combines large-scale, replicated experimental evolution with toxicological assays and genomic analyses to connect anthropogenic exposure histories to organismal, demographic, and population-level outcomes.** A defining feature of my work is the use of manipulative, multi-generational field experiments that impose realistic chemical stress regimes, allowing direct tests of how populations respond to chronic exposure under ecologically relevant conditions. My work aims to improve our capacity to anticipate long-term outcomes of anthropogenic contamination beyond short-term toxicity thresholds.
